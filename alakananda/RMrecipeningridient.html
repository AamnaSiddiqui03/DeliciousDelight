Rasmalai Recipe

Recipe:

  • 1 liter of full-fat milk
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice or vinegar
  • 3 cups water
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • A few strands of saffron
  • Chopped nuts for garnishing (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Heat the milk in a heavy-bottomed pan over medium heat. Bring it to a boil.
  2. Once the milk starts boiling, add lemon juice or vinegar slowly while stirring continuously. This will cause the milk to curdle.
  3. Once the milk has curdled, turn off the heat and let it sit for 5 minutes.
  4. Now, strain the curdled milk through a muslin cloth and wash it under running water to remove the sourness of the lemon or vinegar.
  5. Squeeze out all the excess water from the paneer and knead it for a few minutes until it becomes smooth and soft.
  6. Divide the paneer into small equal-sized balls and flatten them slightly.
  7. In a separate pan, add water, sugar, cardamom powder, and saffron strands. Bring it to a boil and let the sugar dissolve completely.
  8. Now, add the paneer balls to the sugar syrup and cook on medium heat for 10-12 minutes.
  9. Once the paneer balls have doubled in size and become spongy, turn off the heat and let them cool down completely.
  10. Garnish with chopped nuts and serve chilled.
